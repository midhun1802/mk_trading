📖 How to Read This Page Guide ▼
🔑 Key Concepts
Positive Gamma — Dealers dampen moves. Price pins near key strikes. Favor mean-reversion trades.

Negative Gamma — Dealers amplify moves. Breakouts are explosive. Favor momentum trades.

Call Wall — Strong resistance. Dealers sell rallies here. Price rejects or needs huge volume to break.

Put Wall — Strong support. Dealers buy dips here. Price bounces or needs panic to break.

Zero Gamma — The flip point. Above = positive gamma (mean revert). Below = negative gamma (trend).

Pin — Strike with max GEX. Price gravitates here by expiry (like a magnet).
📊 Reading the Panels
Strategy Card — What to trade TODAY. Entry, target, and invalidation based on GEX structure.

Signal Panel Forces — Visual bars showing alignment of 6 factors. All green = high conviction. Mixed = wait.

Hedging Windows — Key times when dealers rebalance. Expect increased volatility at these windows.

Pin Risk % — How likely price is to stay pinned near a key strike. High = range trade. Low = breakout risk.

Breakout % — Probability of a sustained directional move. High in negative gamma regimes.

Expiration Breakdown — GEX by expiry date. Shows where dealer hedging pressure concentrates.

Term Structure — Gamma roll-off chart. "Cliffs" = large GEX expiring soon = potential volatility event.
⚡ Quick Trading Rules:
1. If GEX is positive + pinned: sell options premium, fade extremes, target the pin
2. If GEX is positive + not pinned: range trade between call wall and put wall
3. If GEX is negative: trade breakouts with momentum, use walls as confirmation
4. Call Wall closer to spot = upside resistance stronger → lean bearish intraday
5. Put Wall closer to spot = downside support stronger → lean bullish intraday
